Understanding chiropractic care

What is chiropractic?

Chiropractic is a hands-on healthcare profession focused on the relationship between the musculoskeletal system, the nervous system and overall wellbeing.

A natural approach to health

Hands-on healthcare focused on movement, function and wellbeing.

Chiropractic comes from two Greek words which translate to “done by hand”. In essence, it describes a healthcare profession based on analysing the musculoskeletal and nervous systems, with treatment carried out using hands-on techniques.

How chiropractic works

Chiropractic is a primary healthcare profession that specialises in the diagnosis, treatment and management of conditions involving joints, ligaments, tendons and nerves, particularly those relating to the spine.

Treatment uses a range of manipulative techniques designed to improve joint function, reduce pain and relieve muscle tension, helping your nervous system work the way it should.

Supporting the nervous system

Your brain, spinal cord and nerves control every aspect of your body. Proper function within these structures can support better movement, resilience and overall health.

The intent of chiropractic is to restore the body’s natural ability to regulate and heal itself, without drugs or surgery.

✓

Chiropractic is simple, natural and centred around the wisdom of the body. It has helped millions of people enjoy relief and better health for more than a century.

✓

The General Chiropractic Council (GCC) regulates chiropractors in the UK under the Chiropractors Act 1994, helping ensure professional standards and patient safety.

Treatment and imaging

Treatment is often provided during the first appointment where appropriate. In some cases, further imaging may be recommended before treatment begins.

If clinically required, X-rays can be taken at the Enfield Chiropractic Clinic using the clinic’s digital X-ray machine. Chiropractors are trained in radiography and radiology, allowing them to both take and interpret X-rays.

Safe, personalised care

Chiropractic treatment is considered very safe in skilled hands because risk factors are identified before treatment begins, allowing techniques to be tailored to each individual.

Many patients experience immediate relief after treatment. Some people may feel temporary soreness in treated areas, though this usually settles quickly as the body adapts and improves.
